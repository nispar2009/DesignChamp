The Dandi March 1857

The British had monopolized the manufacture and selling of common salt. They also charged a tax on it. Salt is a very basic commodity in all households and a tax imposed even on this angered many citizens.

At one point in time, the dissent reached such a peak that Gandhiji and 78 other followers of his marched from Sabarmati Ashram to Dandi. At the end, on April 6th 1930, Gandhiji made salt—breaking the salt law. This was a very historic moment in the history of the freedom struggle.

More than 60000 people were arrested with regards to this. Gandhiji believed that a satyagraha regarding salt could help connect with even the poor very well. This salt affected both the rich and the poor—both the Hindus and the Muslims; so Gandhiji also hoped it would bring about unity in the citizens.
