The Quit India movement

The Quit India moveent appealed to the British to leave their Asian and African colonies (mainly India) in peace. This movement was started on August 8th 1942. Mahatma Gandhi created the slogan "Do or die" for this cause.

Intense protests were carried out nationwide. Exactly the day after the movement was started, leaders such as Mahatma Gandhi were arrested. This sparked off even more agitated protests.

As a part of the movement, goverment buildings were destroyed. Public places were picketed. The British employed all their might and tried to bring the movement to a halt. This movement turned out to be a very significant area of the freedom struggle
